Suns & Stars

The Sun is a star, just like the ones we see at night, but it looks brighter because it’s the closest to Earth at about 150 million kilometers away. Stars, including our Sun, are huge balls of hot plasma powered by nuclear fusion, turning hydrogen into helium and releasing light and heat. They come in many sizes and colors, with lifespans that can range from millions to billions of years.

Planets

Planets are large bodies that orbit stars, and in our Solar System there are eight of them revolving around the Sun. Unlike stars, planets do not produce their own light—they reflect the Sun’s light. They come in two main types: rocky planets like Earth and Mars, and gas giants like Jupiter and Saturn. Each planet has unique features, from rings and storms to mountains and oceans, making them some of the most diverse objects in space.

Galaxies

Galaxies are vast collections of stars, planets, gas, dust, and dark matter held together by gravity. Our home galaxy, the Milky Way, contains hundreds of billions of stars, and it’s just one of trillions of galaxies in the universe. They come in different shapes—spiral, elliptical, and irregular—and can span hundreds of thousands of light-years across.
